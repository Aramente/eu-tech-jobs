<p><strong>About our Capital, Impairments &amp; Forecasting Team</strong></p>
<p>Our Capital, Impairments &amp; Forecasting team sits at the intersection of our Borrowing and Finance collectives, responsible for developing, executing and managing the models used to calculate IFRS 9 ECL, and credit risk capital through the annual ICAAP. We work alongside our first line credit teams who design the products, decide who to lend to, set underwriting policy, identify customers who need help, manage the portfolio and report and measure credit risk.</p>
<p>We are scaling up our Impairments modelling team to support the rapid growth and evolution of our portfolio. This role is pivotal for the team as the successful candidate will help develop and further enhance our suite of IFRS 9 models (PD, LGD, EAD, macroeconomic models) across both well established and emerging products.&nbsp; Additionally the successful individual will have oversight of model performance, construction of PMAs/model overlays where required, model implementation, and impairment analytics for senior management.</p>
<p>Strong data and analytical skills are a must, as is experience in credit risk modelling and IFRS 9 provision methodology.&nbsp; Prior experience developing IFRS 9 models is desirable, but model development experience in IRB modelling, stress testing or scorecard models will also be considered.</p>
<p>We rely heavily on the following tools and technologies (note we do not expect applicants to have prior experience of all them):</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://cloud.google.com/">Google Cloud Platform</a> for all of our analytics infrastructure</li>
<li><a href="https://www.getdbt.com/">dbt</a> and<a href="https://cloud.google.com/bigquery/"><strong> </strong>BigQuery SQL</a> for our data modelling and warehousing</li>
<li>Python for data science and predictive modelling, and model implementation</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Your day-to-day responsibilities:&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Assist with end-to-end development and maintenance of IFRS 9 models (PD, EAD, LGD)</li>

</ul>
