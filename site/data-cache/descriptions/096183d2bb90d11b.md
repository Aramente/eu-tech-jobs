<p><strong>Where we work<br></strong>Headquartered in Amsterdam and listed on Nasdaq, Nebius has a global footprint with R&amp;D hubs across Europe, North America, and Israel. The team of over 1400 employees includes more than 400 highly skilled engineers with deep expertise across hardware and software engineering, as well as an in-house AI R&amp;D team.</p></div><p><strong>Role Overview</strong></p>
<p>The HR Operations Specialist owns HR Ops for their location and does so with a high degree of independence. You execute complex lifecycle processes, maintain rigorous data standards, drive local compliance, and actively improve the processes you work with.</p>
<p>Beyond your own delivery, you serve as an informal lead for the location: an escalation point for less experienced colleagues, a reliable partner to HRBPs, business stakeholders, and HR Ops peers across other locations. Employees are supported through the HRBPs; your role is to make sure the HR Ops engine behind that experience runs well. This team works across geographies and functions, and siloed thinking has no place here.</p>
<p>Depending on business need, you may also have the opportunity to lead or contribute to global or team-wide HR Ops initiatives, giving you visibility beyond your location and the chance to shape how the broader team operates.</p>
<p><strong>Key Responsibilities</strong></p>
<p><strong>HR Operations and Employee Lifecycle</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Own the full employee lifecycle for your location, including complex transactions such as international moves, cross-functional transfers, and sensitive terminations.</li>
<li>Act as the local escalation point for HR Ops issues, resolving them with sound judgment and minimal need for upward escalation.</li>
<li>Lead onboarding and offboarding for office and data center personnel, ensuring a consistent and compliant experience.</li>
<li>Support the onboarding of new HR Ops team members at your location, helping them get up to speed on systems, processes, and ways of working.</li>
<li>Partner closely with HRBPs, Compensation and Benefits, Payroll, Immigration, and Legal to execute seamlessly across the lifecycle.</li>
<li>Offer guidance and support to less experienced colleagues on the HR Ops team; help build their capability and confidence.</li>
<li>Contribute to team-wide playbooks, standards, and process documentation that the broader HR Ops team can use.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>HRIS and Data Integrity</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Own data quality and governance for your location in HiBob, setting the standard for accuracy and compliance.</li>
<li>Identify automation and AI opportunities across HR Ops workflows; lead their implementation in partnership with the People Systems team. A key team priority this year is replacing manual and repetitive processes with smarter, faster solutions, and you are expected to bring ideas and drive adoption in your location.</li>
<li>Support the rollout of new HRIS modules or system changes, serving as the local operational expert and change champion.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Compliance and Documentation</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Ensure all employee files, records, and documentation are complete, accurate, and compliant with local and cross-jurisdictional requirements.</li>
<li>Maintain up-to-date working knowledge of employment law in your location; proactively flag risks and propose solutions.</li>
<li>Deploy, maintain, and improve HR Ops policies and employee-facing documentation for your location.</li>
<li>Support travel program administration via Navan, ensuring policy compliance and a smooth employee experience.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Data Center and EHS Support</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Support HR Ops for data center employees at your location, understanding the specific onboarding, compliance, and operational requirements of a physical infrastructure environment.</li>
<li>Coordinate with EHS to ensure HR Ops processes align with site-level safety and compliance requirements, including training tracking and incident documentation.</li>
<li>Build familiarity with relevant safety standards such as OSHA, ISO 45001, or local equivalents, as they apply to HR Ops on site.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>What We're Looking For</strong></p>
<p><strong>Must-Have</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Bachelor's degree in Human Resources or a related field.</li>
<li>3-5 years of progressive HR Operations experience.</li>
<li>Proficiency with HiBob or equivalent HRIS platforms, including data governance, not just data entry.</li>
<li>Demonstrated ability to manage HR Ops independently for a location or business unit.</li>
<li>Solid understanding of local employment law and compliance requirements.</li>
<li>Experience contributing to automation and process improvement initiatives in an HR Ops context.</li>
<li>Full professional English fluency, written and verbal; all internal communication, systems, and documentation are in English regardless of location.</li>
<li>Strong judgment when handling sensitive, confidential, or legally complex situations.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Strong Plus</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Experience supporting HR Ops in a data center, manufacturing, or other physical infrastructure environment.</li>
<li>Working knowledge of EHS requirements and how they intersect with HR Ops, including training compliance, incident documentation, and site-specific onboarding.</li>
<li>Experience supporting or mentoring less experienced HR Ops team members.</li>
<li>Multi-country HR Ops experience.</li>
</ul>

</ul>
