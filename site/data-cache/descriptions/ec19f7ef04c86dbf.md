<p>With a proven track record now spanning decades, Ubuntu is now the go-to Linux distribution for x86/amd64 environments.&nbsp; To stay at the forefront of consumer choice, Canonical - the company behind Ubuntu Linux - is expanding its investment into PC platform stability, reliability, and performance.&nbsp; The Canonical Kernel Team - those responsible for the build, maintenance, and distribution of the Linux kernel for Ubuntu - is looking for an individual to take ownership of aspects pertaining to the boot process, BIOS, UEFI, ACPI, and other platform firmware components as they pertain to the Ubuntu Linux kernel.</p>
<p>The successful candidate will be able to demonstrate a strong understanding of ACPI, ideally from the Linux kernel perspective, as well as enough familiarity with BIOS/UEFI to resolve issues as they are reported from PC platform vendors or the community at large.&nbsp; Prior work experience with a major PC platform firmware vendor or active participation in the Coreboot project would be a significant advantage.</p>
<h2><strong>The role entails</strong></h2>
<ul>
<li>Collaborate regularly and proactively with a globally distributed team</li>
<li>Work closely with our PC platform enablement team and vendors to resolve issues involving BIOS, UEFI, ACPI, or other problems involving boot and configuration of the Linux kernel</li>
<li>Become a primary contributor and maintainer to the Firmware Test Suite (FWTS)</li>
<li>Improve tooling and automation for comprehensive verification of Ubuntu Linux on x86 platforms</li>
<li>Submit, review, and apply kernel patches, working with both internal and external upstream maintainers</li>
</ul>
<h2><strong>What we are looking for in you</strong></h2>
<ul>
<li>Well-organised and motivated self-starter able to thrive in a remote work environment</li>
<li>Professional manner with colleagues, business partners, and the open-source community</li>
<li>Ability to communicate effectively in English, both written and verbal</li>
<li>Significant programming ability in C</li>
<li>Demonstrated software development background involving UEFI and/or ACPI, with in-depth understanding of AML.</li>
<li>Solid background with git</li>
<li>Familiarity with the x86 bootstrap process</li>
<li>Understanding of operating system kernel fundamentals, with a focus on ACPI and UEFI interaction</li>
<li>Ability to travel internationally twice a year for company events of up to two weeks length</li>
</ul>
<h2><strong>Additional skills that you might also bring</strong></h2>
<ul>
<li>Python and Bash scripting ability</li>
<li>Prior background with the coreboot project</li>
<li>Ubuntu/Debian/Snap packaging</li>
<li>Experience with Linux kernel patching and debugging</li>
<li>Familiarity with Devicetree, SystemReady, u-boot, or other non-x86 platform bootstrap or configuration mechanisms</li>
</ul>
