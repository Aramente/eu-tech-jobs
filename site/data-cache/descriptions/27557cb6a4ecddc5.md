<h2><strong>About the role:</strong></h2>
<p>As <strong>Senior Data Engineer – Internal Platform</strong>, you will build and own the data infrastructure that powers Parloa's internal AI transformation. Today, business-critical data lives across dozens of disconnected tools with no unified way to access it — you'll change that by creating a single, self-serve access layer that gives AI agents and teams the business context they need to operate. Working closely with the AI Transformation Team and stakeholders across the non-engineering organization, you'll also define the KPIs and dashboards leadership uses to track AI adoption and impact.</p>
<h2><strong>Areas of ownership:</strong></h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Data Platform Infrastructure</strong>: Own the Internal-facing Databricks workspace end-to-end: configuration, compute, access controls, and cost management.</li>
<li><strong>Ingestion Pipelines:</strong> Build and maintain Airbyte pipelines that pull data from Salesforce, Gong, HRIS, finance systems, and other tools on automated schedules.</li>
<li><strong>Data Modeling &amp; Transformation</strong>: Implement the architecture that turns raw data into clean, trusted, analysis-ready datasets.</li>
<li><strong>AI Data Enablement:</strong> Partner with the AI Transformation Team to ensure AI agents and LLM workflows get the right data in the right format.</li>
<li><strong>AI Adoption Measurement:</strong> Define and build the KPIs and dashboards that track AI usage, proficiency, and business impact across the company.</li>
<li><strong>Data Governance &amp; Compliance:</strong> Own data classification, access policies, GDPR/DPA compliance, and pipeline monitoring.</li>
</ul>
<h2><strong>Who you are:</strong></h2>
<ul>
<li>Experience designing data layers that power AI/LLM-based applications and agentic workflows.</li>
<li>Proficiency in Python, SQL, ETL/ELT tools, and MLOps.</li>
<li>Knowledge of dimensional modeling and modern data warehouse technologies.</li>
<li>Ability to comprehend and execute on complex requirements and resolve performance issues.</li>
<li>Ability to articulate complex technical concepts to non-engineering audiences, making data architecture decisions and trade-offs understandable for leadership and cross-functional stakeholders.</li>
<li>Passion for staying updated and driving innovation in data engineering.</li>
<li>Ability to define and instrument Al adoption KPls: usage tracking, proficiency distribution, and business impact metrics.</li>
<li>Understanding of how Al tools consume data – context windows, retrieval patterns, embedding pipelines – and how to optimise data access for.</li>
</ul>
