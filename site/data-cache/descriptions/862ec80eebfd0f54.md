<p>As a <strong><strong class="textBold">Backend Engineer</strong></strong> on the GitLab Upgrades team, you’ll help self-managed customers run GitLab with assurance by building and supporting the deployment tooling, infrastructure, and automation behind how GitLab is installed, upgraded, and operated. You’ll work across <strong><strong class="textBold">Omnibus GitLab</strong></strong>, <strong><strong class="textBold">GitLab Helm Charts</strong></strong>, the <strong><strong class="textBold">GitLab Environment Toolkit (GET)</strong></strong>, and the <strong><strong class="textBold">GitLab Operator</strong></strong> to improve reliability, security, and scalability in production-grade environments. This is a hands-on role where you’ll partner with Distribution Engineers, Site Reliability Engineers, Release Managers, Security, and Development teams to make self-managed GitLab easier to use across a wide range of platforms.</p>
<p><strong><strong class="textBold">Some examples of our projects:</strong></strong></p>
<ul>
<li value="1"><strong><strong class="textBold">Evolve</strong></strong> Omnibus GitLab, Helm Charts, GET, and the GitLab Operator to support new GitLab features and architectures</li>
<li value="2"><strong><strong class="textBold">Improve</strong></strong> installation, upgrade, and validation automation for large-scale self-managed GitLab deployments</li>
</ul>
<h2>What you’ll do</h2>
<ul>
<li value="1"><strong><strong class="textBold">Maintain</strong></strong> and improve the <strong><strong class="textBold">Omnibus GitLab</strong></strong> package so GitLab components work reliably in self-managed deployments.</li>
<li value="2"><strong><strong class="textBold">Develop</strong></strong> and support <strong><strong class="textBold">GitLab Helm Charts</strong></strong> for scalable, production-ready Kubernetes deployments.</li>
<li value="3"><strong><strong class="textBold">Enhance</strong></strong> the <strong><strong class="textBold">GitLab Environment Toolkit (GET)</strong></strong> and validated reference architectures used by enterprise and internal users.</li>
<li value="4"><strong><strong class="textBold">Support</strong></strong> and extend the <strong><strong class="textBold">GitLab Operator</strong></strong> for Kubernetes-native lifecycle management of GitLab installations.</li>
<li value="5"><strong><strong class="textBold">Improve</strong></strong> the installation, upgrade, and day-to-day operating experience across supported self-managed platforms.</li>
<li value="6"><strong><strong class="textBold">Collaborate</strong></strong> with Security to address vulnerabilities and strengthen secure defaults and configurations across the deployment stack.</li>
<li value="7"><strong><strong class="textBold">Build</strong></strong> and maintain automation and continuous integration and continuous deployment pipelines that validate deployment tooling across Omnibus, Charts, GET, and the Operator.</li>
<li value="8"><strong><strong class="textBold">Partner</strong></strong> with Distribution Engineers, Site Reliability Engineers, Release Managers, and Development teams to integrate new features and keep user-facing documentation accurate and useful.</li>
</ul>
<h2>What you’ll bring</h2>
<ul>
<li value="1"><strong><strong class="textBold">Experience</strong></strong> building and maintaining backend services in production environments, especially in deployment, infrastructure, or platform tooling.</li>
<li value="2"><strong><strong class="textBold">Practical knowledge</strong></strong> of Kubernetes operations, including authoring and maintaining <strong><strong class="textBold">Helm charts</strong></strong>.</li>
<li value="3"><strong><strong class="textBold">Proficiency</strong></strong> with <strong><strong class="textBold">Ruby</strong></strong> and <strong><strong class="textBold">Go</strong></strong>, along with scripting skills to automate workflows and tooling.</li>
<li value="4"><strong><strong class="textBold">Familiarity</strong></strong> with <strong><strong class="textBold">Terraform</strong></strong> and infrastructure as code practices across cloud and on-premises environments.</li>
<li value="5"><strong><strong class="textBold">Hands-on experience</strong></strong> with relational databases, especially <strong><strong class="textBold">PostgreSQL</strong></strong>, including performance and reliability considerations.</li>
<li value="6"><strong><strong class="textBold">Understanding</strong></strong> of secure, scalable, and supportable deployment practices, along with observability tools such as <strong><strong class="textBold">Prometheus</strong></strong> and <strong><strong class="textBold">Grafana</strong></strong>.</li>
<li value="7"><strong><strong class="textBold">Experience</strong></strong> collaborating in large codebases and distributed teams, including writing clear user-facing documentation and implementation guides.</li>
<li value="8"><strong><strong class="textBold">Openness</strong></strong> to learning new technologies and applying transferable skills across different parts of the GitLab deployment stack.</li>
</ul>
<h2>About the team</h2>
<p>The <strong><strong class="textBold">Upgrades</strong></strong> team is part of GitLab Delivery and delivers GitLab to self-managed users through supported, validated deployment tooling. The team maintains <strong><strong class="textBold">Omnibus GitLab</strong></strong>, <strong><strong class="textBold">Helm Charts</strong></strong>, the <strong><strong class="textBold">GitLab Operator</strong></strong>, and the <strong><strong class="textBold">GitLab Environment Toolkit (GET)</strong></strong> to help self-managed users deploy GitLab securely and reliably across diverse environments. You’ll join a distributed group of backend engineers that works asynchronously across time zones and collaborates closely with Site Reliability Engineering, Release, Security, and Development teams. The team is focused on improving installation and upgrade workflows, strengthening automation and security, and helping self-managed customers run GitLab successfully at any scale.</p><div class="content-conclusion"><h4><strong>How GitLab Supports Full-Time Employees</strong></h4>
