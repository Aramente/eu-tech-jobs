## Your Mission

As a Test Environment Maintenance Engineer, you will be responsible for setting up, operating, and stabilizing complex automotive test environments across three domains. The role focuses on ECU preparation, flashing, system bring-up, and low-level troubleshooting in both bench and vehicle environments.<br> In addition, you will actively support domain test drives and vehicle-based validation activities, requiring a valid driving license (Mercedes Benz F2 equivalent) and hands-on involvement with test vehicles.

## Sneak preview of your future tasks

<ul><li>Configure, enable, and maintain bench‑based and vehicle‑based test environments for development and validation</li><li>Prepare, flash, and recover ECUs using low‑level flashing methods (DKF, bootloader, recovery modes)</li><li>Perform system bring‑up and root‑cause analysis across infotainment and embedded platforms</li><li>Support <strong>vehicle test drives</strong> for the assigned domains, including preparation, execution, and basic on‑site troubleshooting</li><li>Operate and analyze Android‑ and QNX‑based systems, including filesystem inspection and ADB‑based debugging</li><li>Diagnose HW/SW interaction issues using CAN and Ethernet analysis tools</li><li>Identify, report, and document anomalies observed during flashing, system startup, and test drives</li><li>Ensure structured issue tracking, documentation, and closure</li><li>Create and maintain technical documentation, troubleshooting guides, and knowledge base articles</li></ul>

## Your Profile

<ul><li>Degree in Computer Science, Information Technology, or a comparable field; or equivalent technical training (e.g., system technician / IT specialist)</li><li>Strong Linux expertise (command line, filesystem internals, embedded environments)</li><li>Hands‑on experience flashing Android devices using ADB and low‑level flashing tools</li><li>Solid experience with logging and diagnostics (Wireshark, CAN, Ethernet, DLT)</li><li>Working knowledge of QNX OS and filesystems</li><li>Proven experience debugging software download, flashing, and system bring‑up issues</li><li>Basic scripting skills (Python, Bash / Shell)</li><li>Experience coordinating technical topics across multiple teams and domains</li><li>Valid driving license (F2) and willingness to support vehicle test drives</li></ul><strong>Nice to Have</strong><br><ul><li>Experience with CI/CD and DevOps tooling (GitLab, Jenkins, Artifactory, Jira)</li><li>Hands‑on experience with Monaco and TUT</li><li>Experience in both Windows and Linux environments</li><li>Familiarity with ASPICE processes</li><li>Background in automotive infotainment systems</li></ul>
