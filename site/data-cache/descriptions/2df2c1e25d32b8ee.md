<h2><strong>Your Impact</strong></h2>
<p>We are looking for a <strong>Senior</strong> <strong>MLOps Engineer</strong> to join the <strong>Panda Team</strong> (Data &amp; ML Operations) in <strong>Data &amp; AI Platform team</strong>.</p>
<p>Your mission will be to build and maintain secure ML pipelines in production, transforming how we handle healthcare data at scale. You will work in a feature team developing critical data infrastructure that enables data-driven decision-making while protecting patient privacy across millions of users.</p>
<p>Working in the tech team at Doctolib means building innovative products and features to improve the daily lives of care teams and patients.</p>
<h2><strong>What you'll build</strong></h2>
<p>Your responsibilities include but are not limited to:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Design and implement end-to-end ML model pipelines in production </strong>(LLM and custom models) with robust deployment, evaluation, and monitoring frameworks</li>
<li>Own <strong>data pseudo-anonymization</strong> architecture within ingestion services, converting Tier 0 (personal identifiers) to Tier 1 (anonymized data) while ensuring data quality and model performance</li>
<li>Build and maintain secure data export services with ML-based threat detection to prevent attack vectors (SQL injection, etc.) using adaptive models rather than manual rules</li>
<li>Manage golden datasets and implement production model evaluation frameworks to ensure anonymization quality and system reliability</li>
<li>Build and maintain data pipelines that efficiently extract, transform, and load data from various sources, handling multiple data formats (text, images, audio, video)</li>
<li>Implement automation and orchestration tools using ML orchestration platforms (MLflow, Braintrust, or similar) to streamline infrastructure provisioning and reduce manual effort</li>
<li>Monitor data and ML platforms for performance, reliability, and security; identify and troubleshoot issues proactively</li>
<li>Mentor team members on MLOps expertise and best practices to reduce knowledge silos and build organizational capability</li>
</ul>
