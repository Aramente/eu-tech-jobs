<p><strong>The Role<br></strong>We're looking for a Technical Project Manager who can operate credibly across every function in the Hardware Infrastructure department. This isn't a coordination-only role — you'll need genuine technical depth to engage with hardware engineers, software teams, and data center operations on equal footing, understand the tradeoffs they're navigating, and drive programs forward without needing things explained twice.<br>You'll move between functions as the work demands: one week you might be driving a GPU cluster deployment, the next you're working with engineers to scope out a monitoring platform or untangling a supply chain delay. You're the person who keeps the department's most critical initiatives organized, visible, and unblocked.<strong><br></strong></p>
<hr>
<p><strong>The Scope — Functions You'll touch<br>Hardware Engineering &amp; NPI</strong> - Manage timelines for new hardware development cycles — coordinating between design, validation, and manufacturing partners. Track hardware bring-up milestones and help engineering teams stay on schedule through DVT/PVT and into production.<br><strong>Infrastructure Automation - </strong>Drive delivery of internal tooling programs: functional and load testing systems for servers, DCIM and monitoring platforms covering power, cooling, racks, servers, JBODs, JBOGs, power shelves, and network devices — and the asset tracking and repair management systems that tie it all together.<br><strong>Data Center Deployment -&nbsp;</strong>Coordinate rack deployment, cabling, power and cooling commissioning, and server production workflows across Nebius data center sites. Track dependencies between facilities, hardware, and software readiness.<br><strong>Supply Chain &amp; Vendor Management - </strong>Manage procurement timelines, maintain visibility into component lead times and ODM/OEM deliverables, and surface supply chain risks before they become capacity misses.<br><strong>Hardware Operations -</strong> Drive visibility and process improvements in hardware break-fix and RMA workflows, spare parts management, and lifecycle tracking — from first deployment through decommission.<br><strong>GPU &amp; Network Infrastructure -</strong> Coordinate large-scale GPU cluster buildouts, including server integration, InfiniBand or Ethernet fabric deployment, and handoff to cloud operations teams.<strong><br></strong></p>
