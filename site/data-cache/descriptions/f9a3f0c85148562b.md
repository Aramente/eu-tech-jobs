Fortune and Fortune Media IP Limited are not affiliated with, and do not endorse products or services of GitLab.</em></p></div><h2>An overview of this role</h2>
<p>As a <span class="textBold">Senior Data Analyst</span> on GitLab's <span class="textBold">Enterprise Analytics</span> team, you'll support some of GitLab's most visible go-to-market and executive reporting. You'll work closely with <span class="textBold">Sales, Marketing,</span><strong><strong class="textBold"> </strong></strong><span class="textBold">Revenue Operations, Finance, and analytics partners</span> to deliver company-level reports, go-to-market performance views, and lifecycle reporting that leaders use to run the business. Working in <span class="textBold">Snowflake, dbt, Tableau, and VS Code</span>, you'll turn ambiguous business questions into trusted, well-documented data products that serve as a single source of truth for performance and targets versus actuals. You'll also help improve our <span class="textBold">Enterprise Analytics handbook</span> and core data foundations so strategy, processes, and metric definitions are clear and usable in our all-remote, values-driven environment.</p>
<h2>What you'll do</h2>
<ul>
<li value="1">Build and maintain <span class="textBold">executive-facing scorecards</span>, go-to-market performance views, and new-customer reporting that connect pipeline, bookings, and product usage signals into targets-versus-actuals tracking by motion.</li>
<li value="2">Design performant, reusable <span class="textBold">Tableau Cloud</span> data sources and help shape the underlying <span class="textBold">dbt</span> models so reporting layers are stable, governed, and aligned to single-source-of-truth patterns.</li>
<li value="3">Collaborate with <span class="textBold">Analytics Engineering</span> and <span class="textBold">Data Engineering</span> to improve dbt models that support reliable, scalable reporting for business stakeholders.</li>
<li value="4">Document<strong> </strong><span class="textBold">metric logic, data lineage, and Tableau usage patterns</span><strong> </strong>in the handbook so stakeholders can understand how data products are built and used.</li>
<li value="5">Implement and monitor <span class="textBold">data quality checks and reconciliations</span> across Snowflake, Salesforce, and other go-to-market systems to strengthen trust in company-level reporting.</li>
<li value="6">Partner with <span class="textBold">Revenue Operations, Finance, and go-to-market analysts and stakeholders</span> to define questions, align on metric definitions and pacing logic, and deliver dashboards and deep-dive analyses.</li>
<li value="7">Present insights in a clear, actionable way for both operational and executive audiences so teams can make better decisions faster.</li>
<li value="8">Share best practices in <span class="textBold">SQL, data visualization, and go-to-market analytics</span>, including code reviews and pairing support on high-priority dashboards.</li>
</ul>
<h2>What you'll bring</h2>
<ul>
<li value="1">Strong analytical skills with the ability to frame ambiguous business questions, structure analysis, and translate findings into clear recommendations for <span class="textBold">go-to-market and executive stakeholders</span>.</li>
<li value="2">Advanced <span class="textBold">SQL</span> skills working with large, complex data models, preferably in <span class="textBold">Snowflake</span>, including joining many tables and building reusable queries and views.</li>
<li value="3">Proven experience building <span class="textBold">executive-facing dashboards</span> in <span class="textBold">Tableau</span> or a similar business intelligence tool, including data source design, performance tuning, and visualization best practices.</li>
<li value="4">Deep understanding of go-to-market concepts such as <span class="textBold">new-customer and first-order metrics, pipeline, bookings, Net ARR, go-to-market motions, sales segments, and marketing funnel metrics</span>.</li>
<li value="5">Strong command of <span class="textBold">GenAI tools</span> for daily use in your work and the judgment to use them effectively to improve speed and quality.</li>
<li value="6">Ability to communicate complex analyses in a clear, concise way through <span class="textBold">presentations, written narratives, and data visualizations</span> for both technical and non-technical audiences.</li>
<li value="7">Comfort working in an <span class="textBold">all-remote, asynchronous environment</span>, with a high level of ownership and the ability to drive work forward independently.</li>
<li value="8">Alignment with <span class="textBold">GitLab's values</span> and openness to applying transferable skills from related analytics, revenue operations, or business intelligence roles.</li>
<li value="9">Experience with <span class="textBold">dbt</span> and modern analytics engineering patterns, including trusted marts, certified sources, and documented lineage, is helpful but not required.</li>
</ul>
<h2>About the team</h2>
<p>The <span class="textBold">Enterprise Analytics</span> team is a centralized, cross-functional partner to GitLab's go-to-market organization and executive leadership. The team turns data from systems such as <span class="textBold">Snowflake,</span><strong><strong class="textBold"> </strong></strong><span class="textBold">Salesforce, GitLab, and other business platforms</span> into actionable insights and self-service reporting that support operating reviews and strategic decision-making. You'll join a <span class="textBold">fully remote, globally distributed team</span> that collaborates asynchronously to define shared metrics and deliver reporting that leaders can trust. We document our strategy and processes in the handbook so stakeholders can use data more independently.
