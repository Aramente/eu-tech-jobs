<p><strong>About the job</strong></p>
<p>As a Staff Engineer, AI Security on the AppSec team, you’ll lead autonomous defense for the AI lifecycle. Build multi-agent frameworks and secure gateways while integrating real-time security gates and identity standards. By mentoring Security and R&amp;D to define the MLSecOps roadmap, you’ll ensure a "secure-by-default" future for agentic workflows and resilient AI innovation.</p>
<p><strong>Responsibilities</strong></p>
<p>In this role, you’ll:</p>
<ul>
<li>Serve as the primary subject matter expert for all AI and machine learning security initiatives across security and R&amp;D.</li>
<li>Design and manage AI gateways to provide a centralized control plane for authentication and authorization and rate limiting across all model and tool interactions.</li>
<li>Build and maintain an autonomous security agentic framework that utilizes multi agent orchestration for end to end investigation and alert triage and remediation.</li>
<li>Develop agentic identity models using OAuth 2.1 to propagate identity across trust boundaries and prevent the confused deputy problem.</li>
<li>Help govern the AI augmented software development lifecycle by integrating real time security gates into the developer environment and CI/CD pipeline.</li>
<li>Manage Agentic Security Solutions that secure AI lifecycle and manage AI workloads at runtime.&nbsp;</li>
<li>Author company wide AI security standards and implement these security checks across Twilio’s stack</li>
<li>Implement human in the loop checkpoints and transactional safety protocols for high impact or destructive agentic actions.</li>
<li>Partner with engineering leadership to set the long term roadmap for identity centric security and automated posture management.</li>
<li>Act as a knowledge multiplier by mentoring security engineers and developing secure by default paved road templates for R&amp;D teams&nbsp;</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Qualifications&nbsp;</strong></p>
<p>Twilio values diverse experiences from all kinds of industries, and we encourage everyone who meets the required qualifications to apply. If your career is just starting or hasn't followed a traditional path, don't let that stop you from considering Twilio. We are always looking for people who will bring something new to the table!</p>
<p><strong>Required:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>8+ years of experience in security engineering with at least 3 years focused on AI or machine learning security operations (MLSecOps).</li>
<li>Expertise in orchestrating multi-agent systems with AWS Strands, LangGraph, and CrewAI, specializing in runtime isolation, PII redaction, and defending against indirect prompt injection in agentic environments.</li>
<li>Hands-on experience with AI-specific frameworks (e.g., MITRE ATLAS, MAESTRO, OWASP Top 10 for LLMs/Agents/MCP) to threat model and defend against a wide spectrum of risks, including direct/indirect prompt injection, training data poisoning, tool poisoning, and data exfiltration within agentic workflows.</li>
<li>Proficiency in securing end-to-end AI pipelines, from data ingestion and training to model deployment and monitoring.</li>
<li>Strong communication skills to translate complex AI risks into actionable business logic for stakeholders.&nbsp;</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Desired:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Hands-on experience in modern application security tooling including SAST and SCA and DAST with experience adapting these tools to catch AI specific vulnerabilities like indirect prompt injection.&nbsp;</li>
<li>Expertise in identity standards including OAuth 2.1 and PKCE.</li>
<li>Experience with AI Red Teaming and conducting adversarial simulations against Large Language Models (LLMs) and agentic systems.</li>
<li>Proficiency in at least one general programming language (Python, Go, etc) with experience in container security and workload isolation.</li>
<li>Proven ability to operate with autonomy and drive high impact outcomes in ambiguous environments by identifying and executing on critical projects without predefined roadmaps or direct supervision.</li>
</ul>
