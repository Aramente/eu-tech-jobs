<p>We are seeking a highly detail-oriented and analytical <strong>Sales Compensation Analyst</strong> to join our GTM Strategy and Incentive Design team. As a Sales Compensation Analyst, you will be the backbone of our sales incentive ecosystem. You aren't just "crunching numbers"—you are ensuring that our sales teams are motivated, paid accurately, and aligned with the company’s strategic goals. You will sit at the intersection of Finance and Sales Operations, acting as a trusted advisor who translates complex commission structures into actionable data. The ideal candidate possesses a blend of meticulous analytical rigor and the communication skills to explain a "pay-out" logic to our high performing sales force.</p>
<h1><strong>Key Responsibilities</strong></h1>
<h2><strong>Compensation Administration and Processing</strong></h2>
<ul>
<li>Manage the end-to-end monthly/quarterly commission cycle, ensuring accurate and timely payouts for the global sales force.</li>
<li>Identify "bottlenecks" in the current payout workflow and implement automated solutions to reduce manual errors.</li>
<li>Act as the primary point of contact for sales inquiries regarding compensation. Research and resolve discrepancies with a high level of professional empathy.</li>
<li>Ensure compliance with all internal controls, company policies, and external regulations related to sales compensation.</li>
<li>Maintain rigorous documentation for audit purposes (SOX compliance) and ensure all payments adhere to internal policies.</li>
</ul>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2><strong>Collaboration and Improvement</strong></h2>
<ul>
<li>Partner closely with the Sales Operations, HR, and Accounting teams to ensure seamless end-to-end process management.</li>
<li>Identify and implement process improvements within the sales compensation function to enhance efficiency and accuracy.</li>
<li>Participate in testing and implementation of system upgrades and new compensation tools.</li>
<li>Provide training and documentation on compensation processes and tools to stakeholders.</li>
</ul>
<h1><strong>Qualifications</strong></h1>
<h2><strong>Required Experience and Skills</strong></h2>
<ul>
<li>Experience: Minimum 2–3 years of professional experience in Finance, Sales Operations, or Revenue Operations.</li>
<li>Domain Knowledge: Direct experience in Sales Compensation (Incentive Compensation Management) is a significant advantage.</li>
<li>Strong proficiency in Microsoft Excel. You don't just use Excel; you make it sing. Nested formulas, XLOOKUPs, and Pivot Tables are your second language.</li>
<li>Experience with a Sales Performance Management (SPM) system (e.g., Xactly, Varicent, CallidusCloud) is an added advantage.</li>
<li>Exceptional attention to detail and a high level of accuracy in data management and calculations.</li>
<li>Strong analytical and problem-solving skills with the ability to interpret complex data and present findings clearly.</li>
<li>Ability to explain complex commission calculations in simple terms and handle sensitive pay-related conversations with patience, clarity, and professionalism.</li>
</ul>
<h2><strong>Preferred Skills</strong></h2>
<ul>
<li>Experience navigating the complexities of a high-growth, international environment.</li>
<li>Working knowledge with CRM systems (e.g., Salesforce).</li>
<li>Ability to build "at-a-glance" reports or dashboards in Excel</li>
