</div></div><h3><strong>About the Role</strong></h3>
<p>We are seeking a highly motivated and experienced Hardware Compute Technical Program Manager (TPM) to join our dynamic team focused on the New Product Introduction (NPI) of our next-generation GPU hardware. In this pivotal role, you will be instrumental in leading the cross-functional efforts from concept to mass production, ensuring the timely, high-quality, and cost-effective delivery of our innovative GPU products that power the future of AI.</p>
<p>You will navigate the complexities of hardware development cycles, collaborate with world-class engineers, and influence strategic decisions to bring groundbreaking technology to market.</p>
<h3><strong>The Technical Program Manager will:</strong></h3>
<ul>
<li>Lead and drive the end-to-end NPI programs for next-generation GPU hardware, from initial concept and architecture definition through design, validation, manufacturing, and production ramp.</li>
<li>Develop comprehensive program plans, roadmaps, and schedules, defining key milestones, dependencies, and critical paths.</li>
<li>Partner closely with Product Management, Hardware Design, Firmware, Software, Supply Chain, Manufacturing, Quality, and Operations teams to ensure alignment and seamless execution.</li>
<li>Anticipate and identify potential risks, proactively develop mitigation strategies, and drive timely resolution of technical and logistical challenges.</li>
<li>Manage and track program progress against established timelines, budget, and quality metrics.</li>
<li>Facilitate regular cross-functional meetings, design reviews, and status updates, ensuring clear communication and accountability across all stakeholders.</li>
<li>Communicate program status, risks, and critical decisions to senior leadership and executive stakeholders with clarity and conciseness.</li>
<li>Foster a culture of transparency, collaboration, and continuous improvement within the NPI process.</li>
<li>Engage in technical discussions with engineering teams, challenge assumptions, and contribute to problem-solving.</li>
</ul>
<h3><strong>Minimum Qualifications</strong></h3>
<ul>
<li>Bachelor's degree in Electrical Engineering, Computer Engineering, or a related technical field.</li>
<li>10+ years of experience in technical program management within the hardware industry, with a strong focus on New Product Introduction (NPI).</li>
<li>Demonstrated experience delivering complex hardware products from concept to mass production.</li>
<li>Solid understanding of hardware development lifecycles.</li>
<li>Proven ability to lead cross-functional teams, influence without direct authority, and drive consensus in a fast-paced environment.</li>
<li>Exceptional communication, interpersonal, and presentation skills.</li>
<li>Strong understanding of GPU architectures, semiconductor fabrication processes, board design, power delivery, thermal management, and system-level integration.</li>
<li>Proficiency in program management tools (e.g., Jira, Confluence, Sheet).</li>
</ul>
<h3><strong>Preferred Qualifications</strong></h3>
<ul>
<li>Master's degree in Engineering or an MBA.</li>
<li>Experience with GPU or other high-performance compute architecture NPI.</li>
<li>Experience working with international manufacturing partners and supply chains.</li>
<li>Strong understanding of PDM/PLM systems and quality management processes.</li>
<li>Experience with agile methodologies in a hardware development context.</li>
<li>Familiarity with industry standards and regulatory compliance for hardware products.</li>
</ul>
