<p>We are hiring a Senior Design Researcher for our Design Team.&nbsp;</p>
<p>User Science is an evolving design discipline at Canonical. While we have long incorporated diverse research on user needs into our products, the mission of the newly formed User Science team is to expand beyond traditional models of UX research, integrating user needs end-to-end in development processes and quality standards across the company.&nbsp;</p>
<p>This mission requires skillful advocacy as well as organizational learning and change. As process pioneers, we draw insights from others, foster collaborative user research cultures, and constantly innovate with frameworks and standards.</p>
<p>This role sits in the design team reporting to the User Science Manager.</p>
<p>Location: <em>This role will be based remotely in the EMEA region.</em></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><strong>The role entails</strong></h3>
<ul>
<li>Conducting user research: Developing user insights in diverse formats, identifying and championing user needs across product areas.&nbsp;</li>
<li>Sharing findings concisely and skillfully: Supporting different teams with well-matched research presentations and clear documentation systems.</li>
<li>Promoting and shaping a data-informed culture: Working closely with process, product, and engineering teams to integrate user research into existing decision-making flows.</li>
<li>Building research scalability and interoperability: Owning research ops infrastructure, making key methodologies more accessible, actionable, and repeatable.</li>
<li>Fostering a learning culture: Contributing to the evolving definition of User Science through user-centered training, resource development, and outreach.<br><br></li>
</ul>
<h3><br><strong>What we are looking for in you</strong></h3>
<ul>
<li>Exceptional academic track record from both high school and university</li>
<li>Bachelor’s or Master’s degree in a related field such as Design, Human-Computer Interaction, Engineering or similar, or a compelling narrative about your alternative chosen path.</li>
<li>Deep research expertise: Proficiency in both qualitative and quantitative user research methodologies, including user interviews, usability testing, A/B testing, surveys, and data analysis. Exceptional depth in either qualitative or quantitative skills.</li>
<li>Strong technical curiosity: An alignment with and interest in open-source culture and the ability to engage deeply with technical subjects and stakeholders.</li>
<li>Proficiency in visual design and/or information design: Capability to translate complex data or ideas into strong, convincing visuals, ensuring that insights are both accessible and memorable.</li>
<li>A talent for storytelling and collaboration: Outstanding ability to craft narratives that effectively explain research findings and resonate with diverse audiences.</li>
<li>A knack for turning chaos into clarity: Meticulous organization and archivist skills, with the ability to transform scattered findings or disparate processes into cohesive, structured knowledge systems.</li>
<li>Portfolio: A robust portfolio showcasing a range of user research and/or design projects that highlight the ability to derive actionable insights from research and directly influence 1) product design and strategy or 2) organizational change.<br><br></li>
</ul>
