<h4><strong>Your Impact </strong></h4>
<p>The Axon Guidance, Navigation &amp; Control (GNC) team in Brussels 🇧🇪 in the Robotics New Ventures pillar supports critical drone and robotics offerings for Special Forces and Law Enforcement. These robots work together to provide tactical reconnaissance and an advantage during critical, high-risk and stressful missions. The product portfolio includes aerial drones (Thor, Scout) and ground robots (Sigyn) — please see: <a href="https://www.axon.com/products/axon-sky-hero">https://www.axon.com/products/axon-sky-hero</a>. <br><br>We are actively working on truly disruptive next-generation drone &amp; robotics technology that we strongly believe will advance our mission to Protect Life.<br><br>The GNC team is responsible for the software stack that makes these robots fly, communicate, and operate reliably. This includes flight modes, mission logic, integration with the PX4 flight controller, and the middleware and services that tie the system together — all running on Linux in Rust and C++ with ROS 2.<br><br>As a Senior Robotics Software Engineer I on the GNC team, you'll be a core contributor to this stack. You'll design and build performance-sensitive software across flight modes, communication with ground control stations, telemetry, sensor integration and processing, and the middleware that connects it all. You'll improve the reliability of existing systems and help lay the software foundations for future autonomy capabilities.<br><br>This is a software engineering role first — you'll bring robotics domain knowledge and apply it to building a robust, well-architected platform. You'll collaborate closely with firmware, computer vision, and imaging teams across Belgium and Finland, as well as US-based teams for architecture and integration planning.<br><br>Working on these next-generation robotics products is not only a career-defining accomplishment, but also a real opportunity to help reach our Moonshot goal: cut gun-related deaths between police and the public by 50% in 10 years.<br><br><br><strong>What You’ll Do </strong><br><br><strong>Location:</strong><strong> </strong>Brussels, Belgium<br><strong>Reports to: </strong>Engineering Manager</p>
<ul>
<li>Design and develop performance-sensitive software in Rust and C++</li>
<li>Architect and implement flight modes, mission logic, and middleware services on ROS 2</li>
<li>Integrate with the PX4 flight controller and contribute to its configuration and extension</li>
<li>Identify and drive improvements to software quality, reliability, and architecture</li>
<li>Debug and resolve complex systems issues across the stack — from inter-process communication to soft real-time performance</li>
<li>Collaborate with firmware, computer vision, and imaging teams on cross-functional features</li>
<li>Participate in design discussions and code reviews as a senior technical contributor</li>
<li>Support flight testing and iterate based on real-world results</li>
<li>Contribute to build systems, CI/CD pipelines, and testing infrastructure</li>
</ul>

<h4><strong>What You Bring</strong></h4>
<p>Candidates with active robotics or drone experience writing performance-sensitive software on Linux will be prioritized. We are looking for a strong software engineer first who brings robotics domain knowledge.</p>
<ul>
<li>Bachelor's or Master's Degree in Computer Science, Robotics, Aerospace Engineering, Electrical Engineering, or equivalent</li>
<li>8+ years of professional experience in systems-level software engineering</li>
<li>Strong experience with Rust, C++, or C — writing performance-sensitive code that manages memory deliberately and handles concurrency correctly</li>
<li>Strong Linux experience as a development and deployment platform</li>
<li>Solid understanding of systems fundamentals: memory management, inter-process communication, multithreading, and synchronization</li>
<li>Professional experience working on robotics or drone systems</li>
<li>Experience with ROS 2</li>
<li>Effective communicator who can articulate technical trade-offs across teams and time-zones</li>
<li>Proficiency in English is required; French or Dutch is a plus</li>
</ul>
<p><br>Any experience with the following is a plus:</p>
<ul>
<li>PX4, ArduPilot, Betaflight, or similar flight controller stacks</li>
<li>Communication protocols / middlewares such as MAVLink, DDS, or Zenoh</li>
<li>Path planning, autonomous navigation, or SLAM</li>
<li>Simulation tools such as Gazebo, Mujoco, Isaac Sim, SITL, or HITL environments</li>
<li>CI/CD pipelines and build systems (CMake, Cargo, Bazel)</li>
<li>Yocto, Buildroot, or similar embedded Linux build systems</li>
<li>Hands-on drone or UAV experience (personal, academic, or professional)</li>
</ul>
