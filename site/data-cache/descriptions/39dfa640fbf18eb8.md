<p><strong>Role Overview:</strong></p>
<p>We are seeking a Product Analyst to take ownership of key metrics, reporting, and experimentation within Nanonets. Working directly with our founders, this role involves goal setting, tracking, and prioritizing features based on metrics-driven insights. As a Product Analyst, you’ll be critical to helping teams set data-informed goals, clarifying the key metrics that drive success, and enabling a hypothesis-driven approach to product development.</p>
<p><strong>Key Responsibilities</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Metrics Ownership: Manage and report on company metrics, setting measurable goals and tracking progress against them.</li>
<li>Outcome Definition: Help teams define data-backed goals and establish measurable outcomes, ensuring efforts align with strategic company objectives.</li>
<li>Input Metrics Clarity: Identify and communicate the key levers that drive output metrics, providing teams with actionable insights.</li>
<li>Hypothesis-Driven Development: Build systems that support hypothesis testing through product features, creating, running, and measuring A/B tests and experiments to validate customer behavior hypotheses.</li>
<li>Product Development Optimization: Partner with product teams to establish high-quality goals, focus on the most impactful metrics, and support agile development based on data-driven decision-making.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>What We’re Looking For</strong></p>
<p><strong>Education</strong>:</p>
<ul>
<li>Bachelor’s degree preferable from a Tier 1 college.</li>
<li>2- 4 years of relevant experience, such as Data Analyst or Product Analyst, ideally within a B2B SaaS company.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Technical Skills</strong>:</p>
<ul>
<li>Proficiency in SQL (required).</li>
<li>Strong analytical skills and understanding of key product metrics.</li>
<li>Willingness to learn Python and enhance data engineering skills.</li>
<li>Robust product sense, with the ability to interpret and act on data for feature prioritization.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Mindset</strong>: Curious, experiment-driven, and eager to tackle complex product challenges through data. Comfortable scrapping or iterating on underperforming features based on data.</p>
