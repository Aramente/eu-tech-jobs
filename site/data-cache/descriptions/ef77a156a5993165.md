<h4><strong>WHO WE ARE:</strong></h4>
<p>SumUp enables businesses to get paid easily, process orders quickly, sell online instantly, and have full control over their money. We create the tools businesses need to make their business and their customer experience thrive. And we love it!</p>
<h4><strong>Team description</strong></h4>
<p>The Payments tribe is at the heart of what SumUp does - processing payments for over 4 million small businesses across 37 markets. Within the Processing product family, the analytics function plays a critical role in helping product and engineering teams understand what's working, what isn't, and where the biggest opportunities lie. Right now, we have rich datasets and a growing analytical foundation, but the real challenge is turning that complexity into clear, trustworthy narratives that help people make faster, smarter decisions. This role aims to help us build a more resilient, scalable data culture within one of SumUp's most business-critical domains.</p>
<p><strong>What you'll do</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Redesign and simplify key Tableau dashboards so that non-expert stakeholders can answer "how are we doing?" and "why?" without needing analytical support</li>
<li>Define and iterate on KPI structures - establishing what to show, what to exclude, and how to sequence metrics so the story is immediately understandable</li>
<li>Build a lightweight insights cadence: recurring updates that interpret what changed, what it likely means, and what follow-up actions to consider</li>
<li>Establish scalable dashboard templates and self-serve patterns to keep reporting consistent and trustworthy as the team grows</li>
<li>Ramp into deep domain knowledge across Payments Processing to proactively surface risks, opportunities, and recommendations that shape product priorities</li>
</ul>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h4><strong>You'll be great for this role if you have:</strong></h4>
<ul>
<li>Strong hands-on experience with Tableau (or a comparable BI tool), backed by a portfolio that shows how dashboard complexity has been reduced through smart structure, drill-down design, and metric hierarchy</li>
<li>Solid SQL skills for analysis and validation, with comfort reading and writing queries independently across large datasets</li>
<li>Experience selecting and refining KPIs, including understanding tradeoffs, avoiding vanity metrics, and aligning metric choices to business goals</li>
<li>Excellent ability to translate ambiguous questions into measurable metrics and communicate findings clearly to non-technical stakeholders</li>
<li>A product mindset when it comes to data — treating dashboards as something with an audience, a primary question, and a cognitive load to actively manage</li>
</ul>
