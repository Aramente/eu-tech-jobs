<p>You will shape the behavior planning architecture for Fernride’s unmanned vehicles across highly diverse Operational Design Domains (ODDs)—from structured tarmac roads to forest paths and fully off-road terrain. As part of the Motion Control and Planning team, you will define how vehicles decide what to do next, balancing safety, performance, robustness, and scalability.</p>
<p>The role sits at the intersection of classical motion planning, control, and emerging AI-based approaches, including end-to-end and learning-based systems and foundation models for complex reasoning. You will work closely with perception and vehicle platform teams to ensure that planned behavior is executable, explainable, and well-integrated with real vehicles.</p>
<h2>How you will leave your footprint</h2>
<ul>
<li>Design and evolve the behavior planning architecture for unmanned vehicles operating in very different and challenging ODDs</li>
<li>Combine classical behavior and motion planning approaches with learning-based or foundation model-based planning, or end-to-end methods where they provide clear benefits</li>
<li>Define clear interfaces between perception, planning, and control to ensure predictable and safe vehicle behavior</li>
<li>Drive architectural decisions that balance performance, safety, and long-term maintainability</li>
<li>Bridge the gap between research and production by evaluating, prototyping, and productizing AI-based planning solutions</li>
</ul>
<h2>What you will bring to the team</h2>
<h3><strong>Required skills</strong></h3>
<ul>
<li>Strong background in motion planning or behavior planning for robotic or autonomous systems</li>
<li>Experience building and deploying robotics or autonomous systems in real-world environments.</li>
<li>Experience working across team boundaries, especially with perception and vehicle or robotics platforms</li>
<li>Deep understanding of system behavior, failure modes, and safety considerations in autonomous systems</li>
<li>Ability to reason about architecture, trade-offs, and long-term evolution of planning systems from modular to unified architectures</li>
<li>Strong C++ skills with experience in performance-critical and real-time systems</li>
</ul>
<h3><strong>Preferred skills</strong></h3>
<ul>

<li>Exposure to generative world models or a desire to apply transformer-based architectures to behavior &amp; motion planning</li>
<li>Hands-on experience with real vehicles or robots operating in unstructured or off-road environments</li>
<li>Familiarity with simulation, validation, and performance evaluation of planning systems</li>
</ul>
