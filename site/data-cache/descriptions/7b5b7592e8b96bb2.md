<h2><strong>Senior Technical Program Manager</strong></h2>
<p>We're looking for a <strong>Senior Technical Program Manager</strong> to take the wheel on some of our most exciting and high-impact initiatives - spanning <strong>automation, AI, and CI/CD</strong>. You'll be the connective tissue across engineering teams, making sure the right things get built, shipped, and measured.</p>
<p>This isn't a role for someone who likes to stay in the background. You'll drive clarity in ambiguous situations, call out blockers before they become fires, and help teams move faster without cutting corners.</p>
<h2><strong>Responsibilities</strong></h2>
<h3>Program Management &amp; Execution</h3>
<ul>
<li>Own end-to-end planning, tracking, and delivery of programs across automation, AI, and CI/CD domains.</li>
<li>Define and maintain program roadmaps, milestones, dependencies, and success metrics.</li>
<li>Run crisp, efficient rituals (standups, reviews, retrospectives) that actually move things forward.</li>
<li>Proactively surface risks and drive mitigation plans before issues escalate.</li>
</ul>
<h3>CI/CD &amp; Automation</h3>
<ul>
<li>Partner with engineering teams to track and accelerate initiatives that improve CI/CD pipelines, release velocity, and deployment reliability.</li>
<li>Champion automation-first thinking across the SDLC — from testing and build pipelines to monitoring and incident response.</li>
<li>Measure and report on pipeline health, lead time, and deployment frequency.</li>
</ul>
<h3>AI Initiatives</h3>
<ul>
<li>Manage programs introducing <strong>AI-driven tools and workflows</strong> into engineering processes.</li>
<li>Track adoption and impact of AI-enabled development tools and automation frameworks.</li>
<li>Coordinate cross-team integration of AI technologies into engineering platforms.</li>
</ul>
<h3>Cross-Functional Leadership</h3>
<ul>
<li>Serve as the primary point of coordination across engineering, product, SRE, and leadership stakeholders.</li>
<li>Build strong working relationships across time zones and disciplines — you know how to get alignment without being the bottleneck.</li>
<li>Communicate program status clearly and confidently to both technical and non-technical audiences.</li>
<li>Drive a culture of accountability, transparency, and continuous improvement.</li>
</ul>
<h2><strong>Qualifications</strong></h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>5+ years</strong> of experience in <strong>technical program management</strong>, with a track record of delivering complex, cross-functional programs.</li>
<li><strong>Deep Jira expertise</strong>, including project configuration, custom workflows, dashboards, and advanced JQL querying, with the ability to leverage Jira data (velocity trends, cycle time, sprint burndown) to drive decisions and proactively identify risks such as scope creep.</li>
<li>Proven experience driving <strong>end-to-end Agile delivery for technical programs</strong>, including <strong>facilitating sprint planning</strong> (capacity planning, backlog refinement, story estimation), daily stand-ups, sprint reviews, and retrospectives that translate into measurable improvements across automation, AI, and CI/CD initiatives.</li>
<li><strong>Excellent written and verbal communication skills </strong>in English, with the ability to adapt messaging appropriately for different audiences, from senior executives to technical stakeholders such as staff engineers.</li>
<li>Strong <strong>project management skills</strong> with the ability to bring structure and clarity to complex initiatives, maintain a high level of attention to detail, and consistently deliver outcomes within defined timelines.</li>
</ul>
<h3>Bonus points if you have:</h3>
<ul>
<li>Experience in SaaS, networking, or connected devices.</li>
<li>Familiarity with agile and scaled agile frameworks.</li>
<li>Background working across globally distributed teams.</li>
<li>Strong grasp of CI/CD concepts, DevOps practices, and the software development lifecycle.</li>
<li>Hands-on experience managing AI or data-driven initiatives (you don't need to write the models, but you need to understand the landscape).</li>
</ul>
