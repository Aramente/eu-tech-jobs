<p>Join us in shaping the future of infrastructure automation for mission-critical law enforcement systems. As a Senior Site Reliability Engineer, you will drive the development of a next-generation infrastructure provisioning and automation platform. This platform empowers engineering teams to self-serve cloud infrastructure, safely and efficiently, while reducing manual effort and operational risk.<br><br>You will have a direct, hands-on impact by building and evolving systems that use automation and intelligent agents to generate, validate, test, and operate infrastructure at scale. We are looking for an engineer with a strong software development background, fluency in languages such as Go or Python, and deep experience designing and operating cloud platforms, who is motivated to improve developer velocity, reliability, and platform resilience.<br><br><strong>Work Location:</strong><br>This role is based out of our London office and follows a hybrid schedule. We rely on in-person collaboration and ask that team members work onsite Tuesdays through Fridays, with the flexibility to work remotely on Mondays, unless there is an approved workplace accommodation. We believe that connection fuels innovation, and our in-office culture is designed to foster meaningful teamwork, mentorship, and shared success.<br><br><strong>What You’ll Do </strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Build robust, easy-to-use foundational platforms and tools that enable engineering teams to provision infrastructure rapidly, consistently, and securely across multiple cloud providers</li>
<li>Write code in Go that is performant, maintainable, clear, and concise</li>
<li>Championing and enforcing Infrastructure as Code (IaC) best practices and coding standards</li>
<li>Employ strong problem-solving skills, with the ability to debug problems in cloud native distributed systems</li>
<li>Influence and educate the engineering organization to adopt new and improved architectural patterns</li>
<li>Provide robust documentation for use by engineers to promote self-service</li>
</ul>
