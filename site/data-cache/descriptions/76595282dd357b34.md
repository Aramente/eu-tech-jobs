<div class="content-intro"><h2><strong>Be Part of Building the Future</strong></h2>
<p>Dremio is the unified lakehouse platform for self-service analytics and AI, serving hundreds of global enterprises, including Maersk, Amazon, Regeneron, NetApp, and S&amp;P Global. Customers rely on Dremio for cloud, hybrid, and on-prem lakehouses to power their data mesh, data warehouse migration, data virtualization, and unified data access use cases. Based on open source technologies, including Apache Iceberg and Apache Arrow, Dremio provides an open lakehouse architecture enabling the fastest time to insight and platform flexibility at a fraction of the cost.&nbsp; Learn more at&nbsp;<a class="c-link" href="http://www.dremio.com/" target="\_blank" data-stringify-link="http://www.Dremio.com" data-sk="tooltip\_parent">www.dremio.com.</a></p></div><h2><strong>About the Role</strong></h2>
<p>As Senior PM for Cloud Platform Infrastructure at Dremio, you will own the strategy and roadmap for the foundational systems that power Dremio Cloud — including the control plane, networking and regional infrastructure, and service metrics and reliability. Your vision is to make Dremio Cloud a platform that enterprises trust completely: always available, continuously optimised, and effortless to operate at any scale, in any region. Customers should never have to think about performance or infrastructure — it should just work, and work well.</p>
<p>You will raise the bar on what enterprise-grade means: from how customers access their data, to how the platform self-optimises under the hood, to how confidently organisations can expand into new regions and deployment models. The best version of this platform is invisible to customers in the best possible way — secure by default, fast without configuration, and compliant without friction.</p>
<p>This role requires strong technical depth, independent judgment, and the ability to engage credibly in technical discussions across engineering, cloud operations, and security — driving alignment and outcomes without needing to own the architecture itself.</p>
<h3><strong>What You'll Be Doing</strong></h3>
<ul>
<li>Own the product strategy and roadmap across cloud platform infrastructure and service reliability, balancing long-term platform integrity with pragmatic delivery.</li>
<li>Define and drive the vision for regional infrastructure and expansion — making Dremio Cloud available, performant, and cost-efficient wherever customers need it, through repeatable and policy-driven deployment models.</li>
<li>Lead internal infrastructure observability and reliability strategy — defining the metrics, logs, traces, SLOs, and alerting that surface meaningful health signals to internal teams and customers.</li>
<li>Drive automation and operational capabilities that allow the platform to scale sustainably — including provisioning, upgrades, capacity planning, self-healing behaviour, and cost optimisation.</li>
<li>Define the operational model for private and sovereign cloud deployments, ensuring consistency, supportability, and scalability across deployment types.</li>
<li>Use telemetry, reliability data, and customer signals to identify gaps, validate improvements, and continuously raise the bar on platform quality.</li>
<li>Collaborate closely with UX Design, Engineering, Cloud Ops, Security, Finance, and RevOps to ensure platform capabilities ship operable, secure, and commercially aligned.</li>
<li>Own documentation of all product requirements, deliverables, and user enablement content.</li>
<li>Drive clear communication across engineering and support teams to ensure product-related issues transition smoothly through defined channels.</li>
</ul>
<h3><strong>What We're Looking For</strong></h3>

<li>Hands-on experience building or scaling a SaaS control plane, with a strong understanding of cloud-native architectures, networking models, and distributed systems.</li>
<li>Experience working closely with engineering teams on highly technical platform initiatives.</li>
<li>Ability to independently engage in technical conversations and influence senior engineering stakeholders.</li>
<li>Experience using telemetry and reliability metrics to inform product decisions.</li>
<li>Understanding of enterprise identity, access management, and governance models.</li>
</ul>
