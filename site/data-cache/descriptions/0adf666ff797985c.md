<p>As a Staff Product Designer, you’ll lead design for cross-cutting platform experiences–the foundational elements that touch every workflow regardless of stage. This role requires deep collaboration; every decision ripples across the product. You’ll partner closely with Product Management, Engineering, and designers cross stages to translate ambiguity into clear direction, define solutions, and ship work that meaningfully improves the experience for developers and DevSecOps teams.</p>
<p>This role will be project based, starting with&nbsp;<a href="https://gitlab-org.gitlab.io/rust/knowledge-graph/">Knowledge Graph</a>, and solving persistent problems that span organizational boundaries and require a holistic view of the product. Along the way, you’ll share knowledge, contribute to our Pajamas Design System, and mentor other designers. Staff designers are key culture carriers who model collaboration, iterative ways of working while pushing the bar on craft.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>What You’ll Do</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Lead design on platform experience</strong> – Drive the end-to-end design process for cross-cutting features and workflows that impact users across the product. Define both tactical deliverables that solve immediate user problems and strategic outputs that connect to longer-term platform vision.</li>
<li><strong>Partner cross-functionally</strong> – Coordinate across multiple groups to align on platform-level experiences. Work closely with Product Managers and Engineering Managers to shape priorities, scope MVCs, and ensure design intent is maintained through development.</li>
<li><strong>Elevate craft and quality</strong> – Demonstrate excellence in interaction design, visual design, and systems thinking. Raise the quality bar through thoughtful feedback in design reviews and by modeling high standards in your own work.</li>
<li><strong>Contribute to the design system</strong> –&nbsp;Actively contribute to <a href="https://design.gitlab.com/">Pajamas</a> by identifying reusable patterns, proposing new components, and ensuring your group’s work extends and adheres to system standards.</li>
<li><strong>Mentor and support others</strong> –&nbsp;Coach Product Designers and Senior Product Designers through pairing, design critiques, and knowledge sharing. Help onboard new team members and contribute to a collective team culture.</li>
<li><strong>Ground work in research</strong> – Conduct usability studies, competitor evaluations, and formative research. Collaborate with UX Research on problem validation and incorporate insights to fulfill user and business needs.</li>
</ul>
<p data-sourcepos="5:1-5:35">&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>What You’ll Bring</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Demonstrated ability to lead design on complex, ambiguous projects that require significant cross-functional collaboration</li>
<li>Strong portfolio showing end-to-end product design work, from problem framing through shipped solutions</li>
<li>Experience mentoring designers and elevating team craft</li>
<li>Proficiency with Figma and prototyping tools</li>
<li>Excellent communication skills–ability to present work, articulate rationale, and facilitate alignment</li>
<li>Experience collaborating closely with Product Management and Engineering</li>
<li>Systems thinking and experience contributing to or working with design systems</li>
<li>Familiarity with developer tools, DevSecOps, or technical B2B products is a plus</li>
<li>Experience working in remote, distributed teams</li>
</ul>
