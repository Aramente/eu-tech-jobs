<p>We are seeking a dedicated <strong>Tax Manager - Product &amp; Operations</strong> to act as our core Subject Matter Expert (SME) for Germany, while playing a critical role in our expansion into key European markets like Spain and France.</p>
<p>In this highly collaborative, hands-on role, you will sit at the intersection of tax compliance and product management. Working closely with senior management, and our operations and product teams, you will help interpret local tax codes, contribute to the design of our calculation logic, assist in the engineering build-out, and act as a key contact point for tax authorities.</p>
<h2><strong>Key Responsibilities</strong></h2>
<p><strong>Product Integration &amp; Engineering Support</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Bridge Law and Code:</strong> Act as a vital link between tax legislation and our engineering pods. Help translate complex requirements (e.g., German <em>Abgeltungsteuer</em> logic, capital gains offset rules) into clear, actionable requirements for product managers and backend developers.</li>
<li><strong>Pan-European Expansion Support:</strong> While your primary mandate is the German market, you will partner with senior management to research, interpret, and integrate tax calculation and reporting logic for secondary markets, with an immediate focus on Spain and France.</li>
<li><strong>Product Build-Out:</strong> Contribute specialized tax input for new product launches. Assist in reviewing tax logic during the design phase and support the testing of calculation engines to ensure accuracy before launch.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Subject Matter Expertise &amp; Ongoing Compliance</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Primary German SME:</strong> Serve as a subject matter expert on German personal income tax, capital gains taxation, and local reporting obligations for financial instruments (equities, ETFs, derivatives, crypto).</li>
<li><strong>Workflow Automation:</strong> Support the continuous improvement and automation of tax-related workflows, helping the operations team minimize manual reviews and optimize cross-border efficiency.</li>
<li><strong>Regulatory Monitoring:</strong> Monitor updates and changes in relevant tax laws across our core and target jurisdictions, summarizing potential impacts on Alpaca's platform logic and end-clients.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Tax Authority &amp; Ecosystem Liaison</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Authority Contact Point:</strong> Act as a reliable contact person for the German BZSt (and assist with the Spanish AEAT) regarding routine product tax, withholding inquiries, and client reporting.</li>
<li><strong>External Coordination:</strong> Facilitate interactions with external local tax advisors, auditors, and outsourced compliance partners across different jurisdictions, ensuring they have the data and context they need.</li>
</ul>
<h2><strong>Required Qualifications &amp; Skills</strong></h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Experience:</strong> 4-7+ years of experience in capital markets taxation, financial services tax advisory (Big 4), or a hands-on Tax/Operations role within a fintech or brokerage.</li>
<li><strong>Technical Tax Knowledge:</strong> Strong, demonstrable knowledge of German personal tax law, particularly regarding financial products and automated withholding models.</li>
<li><strong>Translational Mindset:</strong> Ability to communicate complex tax concepts clearly to non-experts. You must be comfortable working alongside software engineers and reviewing technical workflows.</li>
<li><strong>Language Fluency:</strong> Professional fluency in <strong>English</strong> and native/near-native fluency in <strong>German</strong> is strictly required to navigate local regulations and authority interactions.</li>
</ul>
<h2><strong>Preferred Qualifications</strong></h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>International Exposure:</strong> Experience with or exposure to broader European tax regimes—particularly Spain (IRPF) and France (PFU / <em>Prélèvement Forfaitaire Unique</em>)—is highly desirable.</li>
<li><strong>Additional Languages:</strong> Professional proficiency in Spanish or French is a significant advantage.</li>
<li><strong>Education &amp; Certifications:</strong> Relevant degree in Tax, Accounting, Finance, or Law. Official German tax advisor qualification (<em>Steuerberater</em>) is a strong plus, though equivalent practical experience is highly valued.</li>
<li>Previous exposure to retail brokerage operations, fast-paced tech environments, or international client portfolios.</li>
