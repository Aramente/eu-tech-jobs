<p><strong>POSITION SUMMARY</strong><br>KKR is looking for a Global Benefits Analyst to support the day-to-day administration of our benefit plans across the US, EMEA and APAC. Based in Gurugram and reporting to the Head of EMEA/APAC Benefits in London, this role will focus primarily on global benefits invoicing, billing, eligibility management, reporting and audit support.&nbsp;<br>Working closely with Finance, Payroll, Human Capital and external providers, the Analyst will support the day-to-day benefits operations through detailed invoice reconciliations, data validation and maintaining accurate benefits records. This role is well suited to a detail-oriented individual looking to build foundational experience in global benefits operations within a fast-paced environment</p>
<p><strong>ROLES &amp; RESPONSIBILITIES</strong></p>
<p>• &nbsp; &nbsp;Reconcile global benefits invoices across the US, EMEA and APAC, including headcount validation, premium accuracy checks and resolution of discrepancies with vendors and brokers.&nbsp;<br>• &nbsp; &nbsp;Partner closely with Finance and Payroll to support accurate accruals, cost allocations, funding and timely payment processing. &nbsp;<br>• &nbsp; &nbsp;Maintain and validate eligibility files and enrollment data, ensuring alignment between Workday, payroll and vendor reports. &nbsp;<br>• &nbsp; &nbsp;Perform regular data audits for new hires, leavers and employee changes to ensure accuracy of deductions and coverage. &nbsp;<br>• &nbsp; &nbsp;Support internal and external audits by gathering documentation, reconciling historical data and maintaining appropriate control records. &nbsp;<br>• &nbsp; &nbsp;Maintain benefits billing trackers, renewal calendars and process documentation to support operational governance. &nbsp;<br>• &nbsp; &nbsp;Assist with open enrollment and renewal cycles from a data and billing perspective, including file validation and invoice checks.&nbsp;<br>• &nbsp; &nbsp;Respond to queries related to eligibility and deductions, coordinating with vendors as needed.&nbsp;<br>• &nbsp; &nbsp;Prepare recurring and ad hoc reporting and analytics on benefit costs, headcount, trends etc. to support budgeting and forecasting.&nbsp;<br>• &nbsp; &nbsp;Assist with documenting, maintaining and improving standard operating procedures to enhance accuracy, consistency, control and efficiency across global benefits operations.&nbsp;<br>• &nbsp; &nbsp;Identify opportunities to streamline, improve, automate, and scale processes, incorporating AI-driven use cases wherever possible.</p>
<p><strong>MINIMUM QUALIFICATIONS</strong><br>• &nbsp; &nbsp;Bachelor’s degree in Business, Finance, HR, or equivalent practical experience.<br>•&nbsp; &nbsp; 2-5 years of experience in benefits administration, HR operations, finance operations or a related analytical role.&nbsp;<br>• &nbsp; &nbsp;Strong analytical skills with high attention to detail, particularly in financial reconciliation and data validation.&nbsp;<br>• &nbsp; &nbsp;Proficiency in excel and comfort working with large data sets.&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>PREFERRED QUALIFICATIONS</strong><br>• &nbsp; &nbsp;Experience supporting global or multi-country benefits administration.<br>• &nbsp; &nbsp;Exposure to benefits billing, vendor invoice reconciliation, payroll deductions and eligibility file management.<br>• &nbsp; &nbsp;Experience working with HRIS systems such as Workday.<br>• &nbsp; &nbsp;Strong organizational skills with the ability to manage multiple deadlines in a fast-paced environment.&nbsp;<br>• &nbsp; &nbsp;Clear written and verbal communication skills with the ability to work effectively across regional and global teams.&nbsp;<br>• &nbsp; &nbsp;A proactive, solutions-oriented mindset with a strong sense of accountability and ownership.</p>
