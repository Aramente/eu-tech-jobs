<h3><strong>How you will make a difference</strong></h3>
<p>As an <strong>Android Software Engineer II</strong> on the <strong>SDK</strong> team at Iterable, you’ll contribute to our Android and Flutter SDK and related mobile integration features that enable customers to send billions of personalized messages to people around the world each year. This includes adding new features to our existing integration channels such as <strong>SMS</strong> and <strong>Push Notifications</strong>, and helping build out support for new mobile and device channels we may add in the future (for example Roku, Messenger, WhatsApp, Android Auto, and others).</p>
<p>You’ll work closely with our Product team to refine product features and determine the feasibility of upcoming projects, and you’ll collaborate with teams such as <strong>Delivery</strong> and <strong>User Data Infrastructure</strong> to ensure the products and services the mobile team builds remain fast, reliable, and scalable.</p>
<p>One of our core values is a <strong>growth mindset</strong>, and Iterable is a company where everyone can grow. If this role excites you, we encourage you to apply—we value applicants for the skills and potential they bring beyond a job description.</p>
<h3><strong>You’ll get to</strong></h3>
<ul>
<li data-section-id="t47wr2" data-start="251" data-end="422">Own Android SDKs and associated functionality, ensuring they are robust, intuitive, and integrate cleanly with our Flutter SDK and other cross-platform environments.</li>
<li data-section-id="1hxgiod" data-start="423" data-end="599">Influence technical strategy to build SDKs and integrations mobile developers will love, with clear APIs, reliable behavior, and thoughtful support for Flutter use cases.</li>
<li>Drive team discussions on system architecture and component design, with an eye toward cross-platform usage patterns (native + Flutter).</li>
<li>Design intuitive and performant APIs that feel natural to Android and Flutter developers alike.</li>
<li>Participate in code reviews and help uphold high standards for code quality, testing, and documentation.</li>
<li>Ship code frequently in an iterative environment, balancing quality and speed.</li>
<li>Collaborate with a small, nimble team of engineers that values ownership, autonomy, and learning.</li>
<li>Partner with Product and other Iterable teams to deliver on the team’s technical vision for mobile and cross‑platform channels.</li>
<li>Participate in an on-call rotation to help support our customers (currently about once per quarter).</li>
</ul>
<h3><strong>We are looking for people who have</strong></h3>
<ul>
<li>B.S. or M.S. in Computer Science, or equivalent practical experience.</li>
<li><strong>3+ years of experience in mobile development</strong>, with a strong track record of building and shipping Android applications or SDKs (SWE II level).</li>
<li>Strong knowledge of Android and its core frameworks, along with solid understanding of mobile platforms and constraints.</li>
<li>Expert-level proficiency in <strong>Kotlin</strong> or <strong>Java</strong>, and modern Android tooling and libraries.</li>
<li>Solid computer science fundamentals (threading, memory management, performance, and testing best practices).</li>
<li>Experience building and maintaining <strong>public-facing APIs and/or SDKs</strong>, ideally for external developers.</li>
<li><strong>Hands-on experience with Flutter in a production or near‑production context</strong> (for example, shipping features in a Flutter app, building a Flutter plugin, or integrating a native Android SDK into a Flutter codebase). This experience is <strong>strongly preferred and will be used day‑to‑day in the role</strong>.</li>
<li>Passion for learning and continuously improving yourself, your team, and the developer experience you create.</li>
</ul>
<h3><strong>Bonus points</strong></h3>
