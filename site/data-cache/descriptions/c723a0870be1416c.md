<p>You will work on Fernride’s embedded vehicle software platform, forming the foundation that connects high-level autonomy and teleoperation software to the physical vehicle. This spans the full embedded stack—from bootloader, middleware and operating system up to board support packages, drivers, and vehicles Drive-by-Wire interfaces.</p>
<p>The role sits close to the hardware and is essential for deterministic behavior, reliable time synchronization, and robust sensor integration across the vehicle platform.</p>
<h2>How you will leave your footprint</h2>
<ul>
<li>Develop and maintain embedded software across the full vehicle platform, from bootloader to application-level interfaces</li>
<li>Integrate and evolve Drive-by-Wire interfaces and other safety-relevant vehicle controls</li>
<li>Integrate and maintain BSPs, embedded OS configurations, and low-level platform services</li>
<li>Implement and optimize drivers for vehicle sensors and embedded peripherals</li>
<li>Ensure reliable system timing and synchronization across sensors, compute units, and networks</li>
</ul>
<h2>What you will bring to the team</h2>
<h3><strong>Required skills</strong></h3>
<ul>
<li>Strong experience in embedded software development (C/C++), ideally in automotive or robotics environments</li>
<li>Solid knowledge of embedded Linux or RTOS-based systems, including bootloaders and BSPs</li>
<li>Experience developing or integrating low-level drivers and hardware interfaces</li>
<li>Understanding of timing, synchronization, and real-time constraints in embedded systems</li>
<li>A quality- and reliability-focused mindset for safety-relevant software</li>
</ul>
<h3><strong>Preferred skills</strong></h3>
