## About the role

<p>We are looking for a Lead Product Manager to own our core web presence platform and user experience. This role drives the systems that power our business: platform infrastructure, payments, APIs, provisioning, internal tools,  and especially the interfaces where users actually experience that power.</p><p>Your job is to make a complex platform:</p><ul><li><p>Technical: Rock-solid under the hood</p></li><li><p>CX: Effortless on the surface</p></li><li><p>Commercially smart throughout</p></li></ul>

## Key responsibilities

<ul><li><p>Own the core platform: billing, payments, subscriptions, provisioning, APIs, internal tools</p></li><li><p>Own the experience layer: reseller panel, end-customer panel, and integrations like WHMCS</p></li><li><p>Build AI-led experiences that guide users, automate tasks, and surface the right upsell at the right moment</p></li><li><p>Make complex workflows (domains, DNS, email, SSL, and more) feel simple, fast, and hard to break</p></li><li><p>Improve conversion and monetization across checkout, renewals, and add-ons</p></li><li><p>Reduce support pain by fixing root causes, not writing better help articles</p></li><li><p>Partner with engineering, design, commercial, and support</p></li></ul>

## What we are looking for

<span style="font-family:Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">The ideal PM for this role:</span><ul><li><p>Has at least 5-6 years of experience managing complex SaaS solutions</p></li><li><p>Thinks in systems, not screens, and in outcomes, not features</p></li><li><p>Has a very strong AI-first mindset and approach</p></li><li><p>Has built or owned platforms, not just products</p></li><li><p>Is technically fluent enough to discuss APIs, edge cases, and failure modes</p></li><li><p>Has deep functional understanding of UX principles, and demonstrable experience in building great UX for SaaS products/platforms</p></li><li><p>Is commercially sharp. understands how billing logic, packaging, and UX drive revenue</p></li><li><p>Is comfortable operating in ambiguity and making hard trade-offs (because there will be many)</p></li><li><p>Has experience with hosting/domains, reseller ecosystems, or tools like WHMCS</p></li></ul><p>If you enjoy turning messy, critical systems into something elegant, scalable, and quietly powerful, we should talk.</p>
