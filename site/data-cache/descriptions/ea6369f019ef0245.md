<h1 id="cashcollectionspecialistfinanceoperations">Junior Credit Management Analyst&nbsp;</h1>
<p>📍 Milan, Italy | Office-first setup</p>
<h2 id="abouttheteam">About the team</h2>
<p>The Finance Operations team is responsible for keeping merchant and partner accounts healthy across SumUp's global markets, a function that directly supports the millions of small businesses we serve every day.</p>
<p>As a Junior Credit Management Specialist, you'll sit right at the intersection of finance, client communication and cross-functional collaboration, making sure billing and payments run smoothly and that our clients feel heard.</p>
<p>This is a brilliant opportunity to build a solid foundation in financial operations at a company that genuinely invests in your growth.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2 id="whatyoulldo">What you'll do</h2>
<ul>
<li>Handle incoming requests from Sales, CSM, and recovery agencies, making sure billing and payment matters are resolved accurately and on time</li>
<li>Monitor outstanding accounts, track overdue debt, and keep collection records up to date</li>
<li>Investigate billing history, resolve credit issues, and process payments or refunds with care and precision</li>
<li>Reach out to clients proactively about overdue balances, building constructive conversations that support timely resolution</li>
<li>Collaborate with internal teams to untangle billing challenges and contribute to a smooth client experience</li>
<li>Prepare clear, regular reports on collection activity and accounts receivable status</li>
</ul>
<h2>&nbsp;</h2>
<h2 id="youllbegreatforthisroleif">You'll be great for this role if</h2>
<ul>
<li>Fluency in Italian (C1/C2) is essential; proficiency in English, Spanish or French is a welcome plus</li>
<li>Strong written and verbal communication skills, with the ability to handle sensitive conversations with patience and clarity</li>
<li>Comfort navigating billing processes, financial records, or client account management, whether through study or hands-on experience</li>
<li>Familiarity with tools such as MS Office, Salesforce, or Stripe is beneficial but not essential , what matters most is the willingness to learn</li>
<li>A methodical, problem-solving mindset with a genuine curiosity for how financial systems work</li>
</ul>
