Position: Sterile Compounding Pharmacy Technician

Summary:

• Responsible for preparing sterile compounded medications in a cleanroom environment following USP standards.

• Produce large-volume batches (up to 250 vials per batch) using strict aseptic techniques.

• Ensure all work meets quality and safety requirements.

• Prepare sterile medications according to specific formulations, which may include powders, liquids, or injections customized for individual patients.

• Comply with regulations, maintain productivity, handle and store medications properly.

• Work in the intravenous cleanroom suite, manage inventory, and assist with inspections and quality control.

Essential Duties and Responsibilities:

• Sterile Compounding & Batch Production:

• Prepare sterile compounded medications according to batch records, compounding logs, and approved formulas.

• Compound large batches (up to 250 vials per run) accurately and efficiently.

• Follow aseptic technique and gowning procedures at all times.

• Operate compounding equipment such as repeater pumps, filtration systems, and mixers.

• Quality Assurance & Compliance:

• Maintain compliance with USP and state/federal regulations.

• Perform and document cleaning, disinfection, and environmental monitoring.

• Accurately label and package finished products for pharmacist verification.

• Documentation & Recordkeeping:

• Complete batch logs and compounding documentation precisely.

• Maintain accurate records of lot numbers, expiration dates, and quantities used.

• Immediately report deviations, errors, or contamination to supervising pharmacist.

• Teamwork & Communication:

• Collaborate with pharmacists and technicians to meet production goals.

• Keep sterile compounding area clean and organized.

• Participate in ongoing training and competency assessments.

Knowledge, Skills, and Abilities:

• Strong attention to detail.

• Ability to work quickly and accurately in a regulated environment.

• Ability to stand for extended periods, lift up to 25 pounds, and work in full cleanroom PPE.

Education & Experience:

• High school diploma or equivalent required.

• Completion of accredited pharmacy technician training preferred.

• Active pharmacy technician license/registration in state of practice required.

• 1+ year sterile compounding experience preferred.

• USP Category 3 experience desirable.
