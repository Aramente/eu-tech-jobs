We move with urgency and believe great outcomes come from high standards</p></div><h4><strong>About this role</strong></h4>
<p>We're looking for a Technical Product Marketing Manager to own HackerRank's product and technical narrative. You'll sit inside Product, work alongside PMs daily, and translate what we're building into clear, credible stories for a wide range of audiences: developers, students, candidates, engineering leaders, recruiters, TA ops, and technical buyers. You author the artifacts yourself, you maintain them, and you ship them.</p>
<h4><strong>What you'll do</strong></h4>
<ul>
<li>Develop and continuously evolve HackerRank's product narrative across hiring and upskilling. Connect releases to broader shifts in AI and software development.</li>
<li>Author high-quality content directly: product decks, technical blogs, whitepapers, demo videos, walkthroughs, competitive intelligence, and customer-facing materials.</li>
<li>Lead product release communications. Collaborate with PMs and marketing on messaging and assets for enablement, webinars, emails, webpages, video, and social media.</li>
<li>Maintain and keep product artifacts current: webpages, videos, decks, and positioning materials.</li>
<li>Produce field enablement materials, positioning debriefs, and short-form product videos. Synthesize signals from the field, Kaia recordings, and customer conversations to spot narrative gaps.</li>
<li>Join product jams, roadmap reviews, and sprint demos. Use HackerRank and competitor products daily.</li>
<li>Build with AI tools. Create agentic workflows that help teams move faster.</li>
<li>Partner with the Content Studio on market-facing video. Collaborate on social media and short-form content to promote products and features.</li>
<li>Work across Product, Marketing, Field/GTM, Content Studio, and Support.</li>
</ul>
<h4><strong>Who you are</strong></h4>
<ul>
<li>A product marketer with 4 to 7 years of experience&nbsp; in Product Marketing, Developer Marketing, or Technical Content, with at least 2 years serving a developer or technical audience.</li>
<li>You've written code, built with AI tools (including agents and agentic workflows), and understand technical workflows from the inside. Engineering or CS background preferred.</li>
<li>Strong writer. Clear, technically grounded, opinionated. You can distill complex technology into value-based messaging that resonates across very different audiences. Portfolio of directly authored work required.</li>
<li>High empathy for both the builder (developers, candidates, students) and the buyer (recruiters, TA ops, engineering leaders, procurement).</li>
<li>Comfortable working across IST and US PT timezones.</li>
<li>You take feedback well and iterate fast.</li>
</ul>
<h4><strong>Even better if you have</strong></h4>
<ul>
<li>Experience at a developer tools, API platform, or technical B2B SaaS company.</li>
<li>Ability to demo features to diverse audiences in webinars and live presentations.</li>
<li>Experience across dual audiences: technical buyers and non-technical buyers.</li>
<li>Familiarity with the developer hiring or upskilling space.</li>
</ul>
<h4><strong>You'll thrive in this role if</strong></h4>
<ul>
<li>You follow AI and developer tooling closely and turn observations into content before anyone asks.</li>
<li>Producing a quality deck or blog overnight is part of the job, not an exception.</li>
<li>You want to be in the room where product decisions happen, not downstream of a brief.</li>
<li>Every audience is a different craft, and you find that interesting.</li>
<li>Ambiguity and fast-moving product environments energize you, not slow you down.</li>
</ul>
