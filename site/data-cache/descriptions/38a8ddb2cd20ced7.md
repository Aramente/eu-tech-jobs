<p><strong>About this team and role:</strong></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Mozilla is looking for an Incident Responder to monitor and mitigate attacks across Mozilla’s products and services. In this position, you will be a part of a flexible team responsible for handling security incidents. As such, you’ll need to have years of practical security experience and knowledge of the state of the art for detecting and responding to attacks. You’ll be someone Mozillians across the company depend on and trust to respond quickly and effectively in a crunch, with the outstanding communication and collaboration skills needed to work in partnership with diverse stakeholders. Most importantly, you will become a critical member of the team responsible for ensuring the integrity of Mozilla’s products and for keeping Mozilla’s users safe, within a company dedicated to building a more secure internet.</span></p>
<p><strong>What you’ll do:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Identify and respond to security incidents on a global scale.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Act as an incident commander to drive incidents through the entire response lifecycle.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Design and maintain a portfolio of security alerts, automated actions, playbooks and escalation workflows in support of a high-performing 24/7 incident response capability.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Conduct threat hunting activities, anticipate future threats, and maintain forward-thinking strategies for tools/technology/processes that combat sophisticated threat actors.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Research threat intelligence reports, triage and manage resulting workflows.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Partner with key stakeholders and communicate effectively to maintain a continuously improving feedback loop of preparation, identification, analysis, containment, and post mortem activities.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Participate in on-call rotation.</span></li>
</ul>
<p><strong>What you’ll bring:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">5+ years of demonstrated ability managing security incidents at a global scale and/or experience working in Security Operations Centers (SOC), Product Security Incident Response Teams (PSIRT), and Computer Security Incident Response Teams (CSIRT).</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Expertise with security information and event management (SIEM) systems (eg. ELK, Google BigQuery, Splunk, etc.). Splunk proficiency is preferred.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Expertise with integrating and leveraging threat intelligence for detection engineering.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Expertise with security orchestration and automation (SOAR) platforms such as Tines or Splunk SOAR.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Superb communication and leadership capacity; ability to partner effectively with diverse company stakeholders.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Real-world experience in software development and/or engineering operations for consumer products and services; B.S. in a technology-focused field is helpful.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Practical experience working with cloud technologies (eg. Google Cloud Platform, Amazon Web Services, Heroku, Microsoft Azure, etc.).</span></li>
</ul>
