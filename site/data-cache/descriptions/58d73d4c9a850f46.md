<p>We’re looking for experienced full-stack engineers to join the Figma Weave team. Whether you're enabling real-time collaboration for thousands of designers, building systems that make design tools robust, performant, and scalable, creating new ways for teams to design and share ideas seamlessly, or powering the features that drive product growth, your work will directly impact the experiences of our users—from startups to Fortune 500 companies.</p>
<p>You’ll contribute to our core mission by designing and scaling reliable, secure, and intuitive systems across Figma Weave. <h4><strong>What you’ll do at Figma Weave:</strong></h4>
<ul>
<li>Design, build, test, and ship high-quality features across the stack, using technologies like TypeScript, React, Ruby, Go, and more</li>
<li>Own key parts of the product that impact user experience, internal productivity, or revenue operations</li>
<li>Collaborate with cross-functional partners in product, design, data science, support, and sales to shape product direction and solve complex problems</li>
<li>Operate and monitor the systems you build, with a strong focus on reliability, security, performance, and user experience</li>
<li>Contribute to team culture by mentoring peers, providing feedback, and helping foster a collaborative and inclusive engineering environment</li>
</ul>
<h4><strong>We’d Love to Hear From You If You Have:</strong></h4>
<ul>
<li>5+ years of professional software engineering experience, with a track record of shipping and maintaining complex systems or products in production</li>
<li>Full-stack experience with modern front-end frameworks (e.g., React/TypeScript) and back-end technologies (e.g., Ruby, Go, Node.js, PostgreSQL)</li>
<li>Strong collaboration and communication skills, with experience working effectively across functions</li>
</ul>
<h4><strong>While it’s not required, it’s an added plus if you also have:</strong></h4>
<ul>
<li>Experience with Rust, WebAssembly, or graphics rendering technologies</li>
<li>Prior work on extensibility platforms (plugins, APIs, widgets), or experience contributing to open source developer communities</li>
<li>Passion for crafting excellent developer or user experiences, and an eye for elegant, maintainable architecture</li>
<li>Enthusiasm for learning and teaching—whether mentoring teammates, leading initiatives, or sharing knowledge in code reviews or pairing sessions</li>
</ul>
