<p>Enveritas builds solutions to improve the measurement of sustainability in agricultural supply chains and include smallholder farmers in responsibly sourced agricultural products, in particular coffee and cocoa. A key component of this requires developing and financing projects that directly improve sustainability of production and benefit the livelihoods of smallholder farmers.</p>
<p>We are a dynamic non-profit organization working across 34 coffee and cocoa growing countries around the world, with a remote-first environment. We bring together technology and machine learning with rigorous field processes to collect and process data on supply chain sustainability and environmental impact. This is made possible by a hardworking and diverse team dedicated to delivering impact to smallholder farmers and developing more sustainable cocoa and coffee supply chains.</p>
<p><strong>Summary&nbsp;&nbsp;</strong></p>
<p>Enveritas, working with its implementation partner in Indonesia, is seeking an <strong>experienced and motivated Indonesia Cocoa Agronomist </strong>to support our growing sustainability work in Indonesia. This role will provide <strong>technical cocoa agronomy expertise</strong> to strengthen field interventions, improve the quality of agronomy-related data collection, and generate insights to support reporting, verification, and outcomes.</p>
<p>We are looking for a self-driven individual with <strong>strong knowledge of cocoa Good Agricultural Practices (GAP), experience working with smallholder farmers, and the ability to collaborate closely with implementing partners and stakeholders </strong>across the cocoa sector.</p>
<p>The position is part-time, with an expected commitment of 10 - 20 hours per week throughout the 6-month contract term. You will be based in Indonesia with frequent travel to rural cocoa-growing areas, particularly&nbsp;<strong>Sulawesi</strong>. You will work with a small team of regional and international staff and have the backing of Enveritas’ global expertise in sustainability and operations delivery.</p>
<p><strong>Key Responsibilities&nbsp;</strong></p>
<p><strong>Agronomy Technical Support</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Provide technical advice on cocoa agronomy and Good Agricultural Practices (GAP) for smallholder farmers in Indonesia</li>
<li>Design and develop agronomy-focused interventions, e.g. improving yield, pest and disease management, and rehabilitation practices</li>
<li>Run selected farmer trainings</li>
<li>Train agents on how to provide and run farmer training</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Questionnaire and Tool Development</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Develop and refine agronomy-related questionnaires and field monitoring tools</li>
<li>Ensure survey accurately captures farmer practices, constraints, and sustainability indicators, especially in the context of cocoa</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Monitoring, Verification, and Reporting</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Conduct field-based monitoring to assess adoption of agronomic practices and implementation quality</li>
<li>Support verification and agronomy assessments through field visits and data review</li>
<li>Analyze agronomy-related data, track key project indicators, and contribute insights to internal and external reports (experience with Excel preferred)</li>
<li>Monitor the quality of data collected daily, synthesize insights and report results to the regional team</li>
<li>Support presentations for key in-country stakeholders</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Partner and Stakeholder Engagement</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Support coordination with implementing partners, extension services, farmer organizations, NGOs, and other key cocoa stakeholders in Indonesia</li>
<li>Contribute to mapping and strengthening agronomy-related stakeholder networks in Indonesia</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Required qualifications:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Bachelor’s degree in Agronomy, Agriculture, Crop Science, or a related field</li>
<li>4+ years of professional experience in cocoa agronomy, farmer training, or agricultural development programs</li>
<li>Strong knowledge of cocoa GAP and smallholder farming systems in Indonesia (Sulawesi experience preferred)</li>
<li>Experience supporting agronomy monitoring, data collection, and/or questionnaire development</li>
<li>Ability to synthesize field findings into clear recommendations and reporting inputs</li>
<li>Strong communication skills in Bahasa Indonesia and working proficiency in English</li>
<li>Willingness to travel regularly to rural cocoa-growing regions and work closely with field teams</li>
<li>Proactive, self-driven, and motivated to support sustainable rural development</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Preferred qualifications:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Experience with sustainability standards or certification-linked cocoa programs</li>
<li>Familiarity with climate-smart or regenerative cocoa approaches</li>
<li>Experience working with NGOs, research institutes, or multi-stakeholder cocoa initiatives in Indonesia</li>
</ul>
