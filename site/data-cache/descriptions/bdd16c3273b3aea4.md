<h2>What you’ll be doing</h2>
<p>Day-to-day in this role, you’ll:</p>
<ul>
<li>Design, build, and operate high-performance Android features across discovery surfaces, front pages, and category experiences</li>
<li>Collaborate with Product, Design, and Data to define hypotheses and measure outcomes</li>
<li>Run experiments using A/B testing and feature flags to iterate quickly</li>
<li>Write clean, testable code that’s easy to maintain and ship</li>
<li>Contribute to technical direction through code reviews and design discussions</li>
<li>Improve system quality by reducing tech debt and evolving architecture&nbsp;</li>
</ul>
<h2>Why this role</h2>
<p>Join our Discovery team and shape how users experience Wolt from the moment they open the app.</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>An AI-first team by design:</strong><strong> </strong>We don't use AI as a productivity add-on - we treat agentic coding as a core engineering discipline. We expect our engineers to build with AI agents, to challenge what's possible when the human-in-the-loop is a multiplier rather than a bottleneck, and to push the boundaries of what a small, focused team can ship.</li>
<li><strong>Real ownership, real impact: </strong>Discovery is one of the most critical parts of the product. You’ll own features end-to-end and see your work directly influence user behavior at scale.</li>
<li><strong>Cross-functional collaboration: </strong>You’ll work closely with product managers, designers (including motion), backend engineers, and data teams to build seamless user experiences.</li>
<li><strong>A small team with high leverage: </strong>We operate as a tight, cross-functional team where engineers have a strong voice, move fast, and take responsibility for what they ship.</li>
</ul>
<h3>AI-First Engineering in Discovery</h3>
<p>We're building teams at the forefront of agentic coding.This goes beyond autocomplete or occasional prompting. We’re looking for engineers who:</p>
<ul>
<li>Design workflows where AI agents handle entire task loops, from spec to PR.</li>
<li>Think in terms of orchestration: what does a human review, what does the agent own?</li>
<li>Build internal tooling and abstractions that make AI-assisted development faster and more reliable for the whole team.</li>
<li>Stay curious and ahead of the curve - trying new models, agents, and tools as they emerge, and forming strong opinions about what's genuinely useful vs. hype.</li>
</ul>
<p>If you find yourself naturally asking "can an agent do this?" before reaching for the keyboard, you'll fit right in.</p>

<ul>

<li><strong>Genuine enthusiasm for AI-first development</strong>: You use AI tools daily and have formed real opinions about how to get the most out of them.</li>
<li><strong>End-to-end ownership mindset: </strong>You take responsibility beyond implementation, from breaking down problems into technical tasks to ensuring features are tested, stable, and successfully shipped</li>

<li><strong>Experience operating in production environments: </strong>You’ve debugged real issues in live apps and understand what it takes to maintain reliability at scale</li>
<li><strong>Collaboration and communication: </strong>You communicate clearly and work effectively across teams, including in multi-team development environments</li>
